My interest in coding is rivaled only by my interest in history. Although I have only been programming for six months, I know I have found my true calling. Coding scratches that "puzzle solving itch" I've had every day of my life. In June, 2016, I completed the Python Data Science course at The Iron Yard in Durham, NC. This was no easy feat; I quit my job of three years and dedicated three months, 60 hours per week, studying and honing my skills as a coder in the basement of The American Tobacco Campus. I emerged completely enamored with programming, and am so incredibly excited to explore the many doors of the programming world that have been opened to me.

Prior to discovering the joy of programming, I worked in a variety of fields. This diversity of employment is one of my greatest strengths. I am experienced working as part of a team in an office environment, know the value of hard manual work (and air-conditioning!) and have tons of experience interacting with clients and translating their needs and concerns. I have a master's degree in History from UNC-Wilmington, and the 160-page thesis I wrote is one of my proudest accomplishments. In my free time, I love going to concerts, exploring the culinary scene in the Triangle, taking in a Bulls game, watching a football game or four, or enjoying time with friends.

My mission is to continue to grow and develop as a programmer, find a position that is, like building Fanta-Sci, too fun to feel like work, and to learn a variety of languages and become a true full-stack developer. My training was centered around the fascinating world of data science, but I am equally interested in honing my web-development chops as well.
